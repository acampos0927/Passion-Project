About Joe Madureira

Joe Madureira, often known by the nickname Joe Mad, is a renowned comic book artist and writer, as well as a video game developer. He is best known for his work on Marvel Comics' "Uncanny X-Men" and for his creator-owned comic book series "Battle Chasers." His distinctive style, which combines Western comic book influences with Japanese manga, has garnered him a dedicated fan base and significant acclaim.

Joe was born on December 3, 1974, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He began his professional career at a young age, working for Marvel Comics while still in high school. His dynamic and energetic art style quickly made him a standout talent in the comic book industry.

In 1998, Joe left Marvel to form his own comic book company, Cliffhanger, where he launched "Battle Chasers." Although the series was a critical and commercial success, it suffered from an irregular release schedule and was ultimately left unfinished. Despite this, "Battle Chasers" remains a beloved series among fans.

After his stint in comics, Joe transitioned into the video game industry. He co-founded Vigil Games and served as the creative director for the popular "Darksiders" series. His experience in both comics and games has allowed him to blend storytelling and visual artistry in unique and compelling ways.

Today, Joe continues to work on various creative projects, inspiring a new generation of artists and storytellers with his innovative approach to art and storytelling.
